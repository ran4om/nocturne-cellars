# Nocturne Cellars

## Register

Brand

## Product Purpose

Nocturne Cellars is a fictional small-production winery rooted in the volcanic soils of Mount Etna. The website introduces the estate, communicates the physical intensity of its terrain, presents three current wines, and turns high-intent visitors into private tasting or allocation inquiries.

The experience is not an online shop. It is a cinematic invitation into a place, paced like a short film and grounded by clear, readable content.

## Primary Users

- Wine collectors looking for distinctive, low-volume Etna wines.
- Sommeliers and independent hospitality buyers researching producers.
- Design-aware travelers planning private tastings in Sicily.
- Press and trade partners seeking a concise story and contact route.

## User Goals

1. Understand what makes Nocturne different in under one minute.
2. See the current wines and their character without decoding technical jargon.
3. Learn how the terrain and cellar practice shape the wine.
4. Request a tasting, allocation conversation, or trade information.

## Brand Identity

Nocturne is mineral, nocturnal, and exacting. It is a family estate with contemporary restraint, not a rustic tourist winery and not an ostentatious luxury label.

Voice words: smoldering, tactile, hushed.

## Story

The fictional estate sits at 790 meters on Etna's north slope. Old Nerello Mascalese vines grow through fractured basalt. Grapes are picked by hand, fermented with native yeasts, and raised slowly in old oak and concrete. The founding idea is simple: preserve the tension between mountain cold, Mediterranean sun, and living volcanic ground.

## Content Principles

- Use concrete sensory language: ash, stone, sour cherry, wild herbs, cold air.
- Keep claims believable and specific.
- Avoid inflated luxury language and generic phrases such as handcrafted excellence.
- Never use fake testimonials, invented press logos, or unverifiable awards.
- Never use em dashes.

## Core Content

- Opening statement: wine grown after dark, from living volcanic ground.
- Origin: 790-meter north-slope vineyards and fractured basalt.
- Three wines: Rosso 2022, Contrada Luna 2021, Bianco di Cenere 2023.
- Practice: hand harvest, native fermentation, patient élevage.
- Inquiry CTA: private tasting or allocation request.

## Functional Requirements

- One-page responsive site.
- Cinematic scroll story with frame-like transitions.
- Persistent but quiet navigation.
- Current-wine overview with meaningful tasting language.
- Accessible inquiry form using a mailto fallback or clear contact action.
- Keyboard-visible focus states and semantic landmarks.
- Full reduced-motion fallback with no essential content hidden.

## Success Criteria

- Visitors can identify location, grape style, and production philosophy.
- Primary inquiry CTA remains easy to find throughout the experience.
- The page feels bespoke at desktop scale and remains coherent on small phones.
- Motion supports narrative pacing without obstructing reading.

## Anti-References

- Generic burgundy-and-gold luxury templates.
- Editorial magazine layouts with repeated rules and tiny labels.
- Ecommerce bottle grids.
- Fake scarcity counters, awards, testimonials, and lifestyle clichés.
- Decorative glass cards or neon effects.
